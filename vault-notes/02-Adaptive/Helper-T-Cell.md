# Helper T Cell

> A helper T cell (CD4+) is the orchestrator of the adaptive immune response — it activates B cells and macrophages.

## Key facts

- Recognises antigens presented on **MHC class II** by APCs.
- Subtypes: **Th1** (anti-virus, anti-tumour), **Th2** (anti-parasite, drives allergy), **Th17** (anti-fungal, autoimmunity link), **Treg** (suppressor).
- Without helper T cells, B cells cannot produce strong antibody responses — this is why HIV is so devastating.
- Cytokine profile defines subtype: Th1 → IFN-γ, Th2 → IL-4/5/13, Th17 → IL-17.

## App connection

Allergy entries (sneezing, hives) often correlate with Th2-skewed responses — a future analytics angle.
