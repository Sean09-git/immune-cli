# Plasma Cell

> A plasma cell is a terminally differentiated B cell — an antibody factory.

## Key facts

- Can secrete **~2,000 antibodies per second**.
- Lifespan ranges from days (short-lived) to decades (long-lived, in bone marrow).
- Plasma cells in bone marrow are why blood antibody titres stay high for years.
- Multiple myeloma = cancer of plasma cells.

## App connection

After a vaccine dose, your plasma cells work overtime for ~2 weeks. Worth a learning card.
