# Mast Cell

> A mast cell is a tissue-resident innate cell that releases histamine — the central player in allergy.

## Key facts

- Lives in skin, gut, and airway mucosa — the body's borders.
- Has surface **IgE receptors**. When IgE bound to allergen cross-links → degranulation.
- Releases **histamine, leukotrienes, tryptase** — the molecules of itch, swelling, and wheezing.
- When mass-degranulation happens systemically → **anaphylaxis**.
