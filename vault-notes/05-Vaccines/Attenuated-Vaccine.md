# Attenuated Vaccine

> An attenuated vaccine uses a weakened but live form of a pathogen to induce a strong, long-lasting response.

## Key facts

- Examples: **MMR, BCG, oral polio, yellow fever, chickenpox**.
- Often gives **lifelong immunity from a single dose** — closest mimic of natural infection.
- Risk: in immunocompromised individuals, the weakened pathogen can still cause disease.
- Cannot be given during pregnancy in most cases.

## App connection

Help users understand why some vaccines are 'once-and-done' and others need boosters.
