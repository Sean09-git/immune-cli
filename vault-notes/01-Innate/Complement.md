# Complement

> The complement system is a cascade of ~30 blood proteins that punch holes in pathogens, tag them for eating, and amplify inflammation.

## Key facts

- Three activation paths: **classical** (antibody-triggered), **lectin** (sugar pattern), **alternative** (surface-triggered).
- All paths converge on **C3** and end at **MAC** (Membrane Attack Complex).
- MAC drills a pore in the pathogen's membrane → lysis.
- Opsonisation: C3b coats pathogens so phagocytes recognise and eat them faster.

## App connection

Complement deficiencies cause recurrent bacterial infections — a category the app can flag.
