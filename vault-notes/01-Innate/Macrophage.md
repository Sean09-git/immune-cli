# Macrophage

> A macrophage is a long-lived innate immune cell that eats pathogens, cleans debris, and signals the rest of the immune system.

## Key facts

- Name literally means *'big eater'* — performs **phagocytosis**.
- Acts as an **antigen-presenting cell (APC)** — links innate to adaptive immunity.
- Lives in tissues for months (skin = Langerhans cells, liver = Kupffer cells, brain = microglia).
- Releases cytokines like **IL-1, IL-6, TNF-α** that drive inflammation.

## App connection

Inflammation markers in your journal (swelling, fatigue) often trace back to macrophage cytokines.
