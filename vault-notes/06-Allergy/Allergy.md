# Allergy

> An allergy is an immune reaction to a normally harmless substance, mediated mostly by IgE and mast cells.

## Key facts

- Four types of hypersensitivity: **Type I (IgE, immediate)**, II (cytotoxic), III (immune complex), IV (cell-mediated, delayed).
- Allergic march in children: eczema → food allergy → asthma → allergic rhinitis.
- Rising globally — **hygiene hypothesis** suggests reduced microbial exposure shifts immunity to Th2 dominance.
- Symptoms range from mild (itch, sneeze) to fatal (anaphylaxis).
