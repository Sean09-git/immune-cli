# Histamine

> Histamine is a small molecule released by mast cells that causes itch, swelling, and bronchoconstriction.

## Key facts

- Four receptor types: **H1** (allergy symptoms), **H2** (stomach acid), **H3** (CNS), **H4** (immune cells).
- Antihistamines = **H1 receptor blockers** (loratadine, cetirizine).
- Also a neurotransmitter — H1 blockers crossing into brain cause drowsiness.
- Found in aged cheese, wine, fermented foods — explains 'histamine intolerance'.
