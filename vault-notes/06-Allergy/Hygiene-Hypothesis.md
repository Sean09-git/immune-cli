# Hygiene Hypothesis

> The hygiene hypothesis proposes that reduced early-life microbial exposure increases allergy and autoimmune risk.

## Key facts

- Originally proposed by **David Strachan in 1989** — observed lower hay fever in children with older siblings.
- Modern refinement: 'old friends hypothesis' — we lack ancient microbial partners, not 'germs' per se.
- Risk factors: small family size, urban living, heavy antibiotic use, C-section, formula feeding.
- Not a recommendation to skip hygiene — vaccines and clean water still save vastly more lives.
