# Gut Microbiome

> The gut microbiome is the trillions of bacteria, archaea, fungi, and viruses living in the intestines — central to immune calibration.

## Key facts

- **~70% of immune cells** are in the gut-associated lymphoid tissue (GALT).
- Diverse microbiome → produces **short-chain fatty acids (SCFAs)** → feed regulatory T cells.
- Antibiotics in early life → reduced diversity → higher allergy and asthma risk.
- Fibre-rich diet is the single most reliable lever for microbiome diversity.

## App connection

Diet quality (fibre, fermented foods) field → ties to allergy and inflammation patterns.
