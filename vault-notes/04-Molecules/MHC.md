# MHC

> MHC (Major Histocompatibility Complex) is a set of cell-surface molecules that display peptide fragments to T cells.

## Key facts

- **MHC class I**: on all nucleated cells. Presents to **CD8+** T cells. Shows what's *inside* the cell.
- **MHC class II**: only on APCs (dendritic cells, macrophages, B cells). Presents to **CD4+** T cells.
- Human form is called **HLA** (Human Leukocyte Antigen). HLA matching matters for organ transplants.
- Most polymorphic gene set in the human genome — explains why no two people respond identically.
