# Cytokine

> A cytokine is a small signalling protein that immune cells use to talk to each other.

## Key facts

- Five families: **interleukins (IL), interferons (IFN), tumour necrosis factors (TNF), chemokines, colony-stimulating factors (CSF)**.
- Each cytokine has a target receptor — the system is like a chemical text-message network.
- **Cytokine storm**: runaway cytokine release. Drives severe COVID-19 and sepsis.
- Cytokines are pleiotropic (one cytokine, many effects) and redundant (many cytokines, similar effect).

## App connection

Inflammation cards lean on cytokines — they explain *why* you feel tired during an infection.
